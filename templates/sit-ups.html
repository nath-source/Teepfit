Sit Ups

x6

Lie on your back with your hands behind your ears. Then lift your upper body off the floor and slowly up to the sitting position. Don't tug your neck when you get up. Slowly go back to the start position and repeat the exercise. If your spine hurts, please change to another workout.

Common Mistakes

*Pulling your neck with hands: Pulling your neck during Sit-ups can strain your neck to potential injury. Instead, keep your hands behind your head and use your core muscles to lift your upper body.


*Not engaging the core muscles properly: Relying too heavily on other muscles to lift your body - like your hip flexors or lower back, may lead not only to a less effective workout but also to potential injury.


*Using momentum to complete the movement: Using a swinging or jerking motion to lift your body off the floor significantly reduces the exercise's effectiveness because it bypasses the gradual muscle contraction that truly strengthens the core. Moreover, using momentum can also increase the risk of strain or injury, particularly to the neck and spine.
