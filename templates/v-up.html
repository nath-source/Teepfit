V-UP

x14

Lie On Your Back With Your Arms And Legs Extended And Your Legs Squeezed Together. Raise your arms to touch your toes, then go back to start position and repeat the exercise.

Common Mistakes

*Bending The Knees: Bending your knees during the V-up reduces the engagement of your abdominal and hip flexor muscles, making the exercise less effective. Keep your legs as straight as possible and focus on using your abdominal muscles to lift your legs.


*Swinging The Arms: Swinging the arms instead of keeping them straight and aligned with the body can decrease the effectiveness of the exercise.


*Jerking The Upper Body: This can occur if you try to use momentum to pull your upper body off the ground. This not only reduces the effectiveness of the exercise but can also strain your neck and spine. To avoid this, make sure to lift your upper body in a slow and controlled manner.


*Not Engaging The Core: It requires the core muscles to be engaged throughout the entire exercise. If the core is not engaged, it can lead to poor form and an ineffective workout.
