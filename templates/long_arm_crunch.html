Long Arm Crunches

x12

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Put your arms straight over the top of your head. Lift your upper body off the floor, then slowly go back to the start position. The exercise increases abdominal endurance.

Common Mistakes

*Getting Too Fast Or Using Momentum: This can make the exercise easier. However, this reduces the workload on the abdominal muscles and diminishes the benefits. It's essential to perform the movement slowly and with control.


*Pulling Your Neck: Pulling your neck during Long Arm Crunches can strain your neck and lead to potential injury. Instead, use your core muscles to lift your upper body.


*The Back Does Not Touch The Ground: It's important to lower the torso all the way down until the shoulder blades touch the ground and then lift it up. Make sure your abdominal muscles are engaged the entire time.


*Holding The Breath: Many people tend to hold their breath while performing crunches, which can prevent the core muscles from fully engaging
